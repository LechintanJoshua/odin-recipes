Carbonara

Spaghetti carbonara is an Italian main course consisting of spaghetti (long thin strands of pasta) with guanciale (cured pork cheeks) and a souce made from eggs Pecorino Romano and black pepper.

This dish is probably from Rome.

Ingredients:

  • 80-100g of spaghettoni
  • 50-100g of pecorino romano
  • 50-100g of guanciale
  • 3 eggs
  • salt and black pepper

How to prepare:

  1. Add water to a pot and bring to a boil and add a spoon of salt
  2. While the water is boiling chop some guanciale into tiny cubes
  3. Add around 80-100g of spaghettoni (thick spaghetti)
  4. while the pasta is boiling add the guanciale to a low-mediu heat pan
  5. Fry it until crispy (you don't need oil as guanciale has a lot of fat)
  6. In a bowl crack 3 egg yolks and add salt and pepper (some like the pepper a little toasted) and misk it
  7. Grate some Pecorino Romano over the mixture
  8. Add the boiled pasta in the pan where you fried the guanciale (the heat should be turned of)
  9. While the pasta is still hot pour the mixture of eggs and cheese over and add pasta water to pasteurize the eggs
  10. Whisk until it becomes creamy and continue to add water until the desired consistency
  11. Serve, eat and enjoy!
